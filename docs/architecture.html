Reference architecture

Designing a resilient greenhouse intelligence platform

This page summarizes the target architecture for a modern greenhouse platform that combines telemetry, automation, analytics, and AI-assisted operations into a secure, observable, and evolvable system.

Explore system overview Review principles

At a glance

  • Event-driven ingestion from edge devices and operational systems
  • Clear separation between control-plane, data-plane, and experience layers
  • Operational analytics and AI workflows built on governed data products
  • Security, observability, and reliability designed in from the start

Architecture summary

Platform capabilities organized by concern

Edge and integration

Sensors, controllers, gateways, and line-of-business systems continuously publish operational signals.

Core platform

Ingestion, orchestration, messaging, storage, and policy services provide a stable backbone for workloads.

Intelligence layer

Analytics, simulation, forecasting, and AI copilots transform raw data into decisions and recommended actions.

User experiences

Dashboards, mobile tools, alerts, and operator workflows expose information in context for each role.

System overview

Layered architecture for greenhouse operations

The platform should decouple real-time greenhouse control from business applications and advanced intelligence services, enabling each domain to evolve independently while still sharing trusted data.

Experience layer

Operations portal, agronomist workspace, mobile field apps, notifications, and executive reporting.

Intelligence layer

Decision support, anomaly detection, demand forecasting, digital twins, and AI-assisted workflows.

Platform services layer

API gateway, identity, workflow automation, event routing, telemetry processing, and observability.

Data foundation layer

Hot-path operational stores, historical data lakehouse, semantic models, metadata, and governance.

Edge and source systems

Climate sensors, irrigation systems, PLCs, cameras, ERP, planning tools, and external market/weather feeds.

Design principles

What should guide implementation choices

Operational safety first

Greenhouse control loops must remain safe and deterministic even when cloud services are degraded.

Loose coupling by default

Use events, APIs, and contract-based integrations so teams can release independently with lower risk.

Data as a product

Create trusted, reusable datasets with clear owners, quality controls, and discoverable semantics.

Progressive intelligence

Start with visibility and recommendations, then automate only where confidence, guardrails, and governance allow.

Secure by design

Apply identity boundaries, least privilege, device trust, auditability, and data protection throughout the stack.

Observable systems

Instrument the platform so teams can detect incidents, trace dependencies, and measure business outcomes.

Key flows

Critical information pathways

  • Telemetry ingestion: Environmental and equipment data flows from edge devices into streaming and historical stores.
  • Command and control: Approved workflows send commands back through governed interfaces with policy checks and audit logs.
  • Decision support: Models and rules evaluate current conditions to recommend interventions to operators.
  • Business planning: Production, inventory, and demand signals feed forecasting and planning experiences.

Cross-cutting concerns

Platform guardrails

  • Identity and access management across users, services, and devices
  • End-to-end observability for applications, infrastructure, and greenhouse operations
  • Resilience patterns including retries, buffering, failover, and degraded-mode operation
  • Governance for data retention, lineage, model monitoring, and compliance evidence

Implementation approach

Recommended rollout sequence

  1. Stabilize edge connectivity, telemetry collection, and a minimal operational data model.
  2. Introduce shared platform services for integration, security, and observability.
  3. Deliver high-value operator dashboards, alerts, and workflow automation.
  4. Layer in advanced analytics, simulation, and AI copilots with explicit governance controls.
